# Cyclistic Bike Share Analysis

## Overview
This project was completed as the capstone project for the Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate.

The objective was to analyze historical Cyclistic bike-share data to identify behavioral differences between annual members and casual riders and provide data-driven recommendations to help increase membership conversions.

---

## Business Task

Identify how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently and recommend marketing strategies to encourage casual riders to become annual members.

---

## Tools Used

- SQL
- Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib)
- Tableau
- Google Docs

---

## Dataset

The analysis uses 12 months of publicly available Divvy bike-share data (July 2025 – June 2026).

Original dataset:
- Approximately 5.9 million trips

Final cleaned dataset:
- 5,770,091 trips

Source:
https://divvy-tripdata.s3.amazonaws.com/

---

## Data Cleaning

The following records were removed:

- 29 trips with negative ride duration
- 162,246 trips shorter than 60 seconds
- 12 trips longer than 24 hours

---

## Key Findings

- Casual riders average **22.6 minutes** per ride, compared to **12.7 minutes** for members.
- Members ride most frequently during weekdays, especially Tuesday through Thursday.
- Casual riders ride significantly more during weekends.
- Both rider groups show strong seasonal trends, with peak usage during summer.
- Electric bikes are the preferred bike type for both members and casual riders.

---

## Recommendations

- Launch weekend-focused membership campaigns.
- Offer flexible seasonal memberships.
- Promote discounted electric bike access for members.
- Target frequent casual riders with personalized membership offers.
